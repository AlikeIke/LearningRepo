groups *username* shows you what groups a user is part of

/etc/groups shows the groups on the system, 3 columns. Group name, group
passwords, group ID, 4th column is optional and shows what user are members of
that group

sudo groupadd *groupname* creates a group
sudo groupdel *groupname* deletes a group

2 types of groups, primary groups and secondary group. Its designation is based
on how its assigned. There can be 1 primary group and any number of secondary
groups. The groupID on your /etc/passwd designates ur primary group

#Adds user to a secondary group. -a appends, -G for secondary
sudo usermod -a -G *groupname* *username*

#Changes users primary group. -g for primary group
sudo usermod -g *groupnname* *username*
